Yeg Yard Sharing

Overview

The Good Worm organization is launching a new yard sharing initiative in which those in need can grow food on othersâ€™ land in exchange for a cut of the produce. This project needs assistance connecting landowners with growers in a convenient and meaningful way that overcomes obstacles like location or language barriers.


Design focus

Use various media and languages to make resources and communication easily accessible to all parties such as landholders, growers, YEG Yard Sharing organization.

Other Info:

Team size: 6
(Christine Drummond, Howard Chu, Jeffrey Chen, Lia Seo, Melissa Chan, Spencer Moore)

Duration: Sep 2020 - Dec 2020 (13 weeks)

Tools: Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop

My role: User Interface Design & Prototyping

Process Analysis + Challenges

Creating Personas & User Journey Maps for landholders & growers

My role in this stage:

  • I was responsible for analyzing the survey results and use these data to create the journey maps.

Challenges:

  • The Good Worm organization is located in Edmonton. All of our team members have never been there before. It is hard for us to find potential landholders and growers who lived in Edmonton for interviews.
  • We cannot do face-to-face interviews because of the pandemic.

How we solve:

Luckily, Nicola, the Founder and Program Director of Good Worm, has a list of interested people in this yard-sharing program. We created surveys and questionnaires forms for them to fill out. We also do some face-to-face interviews with our family members who have planting experience or are interested in this program.

Creating matchmaking web application

My role in this stage:

  • I was responsible for building the web application prototype with another team member.

Challenges:

Addressing privacy concerns

  • Determine what contact information is required on the application.
  • Privacy concern of personal address.

How we solve:

We decided to add a guest view for users interested in this program but not ready to register an account. They can only view the approximate location of the potential landholders or growers near them. When users register an account, they can view more information such as name, available time, tools for borrowing.

Reflection + Takeaways

Overall, this project was successful because we completed the design focus we identified. This was the first time I worked in a team of six through online collaboration tools like Zoom and Figma. It is hard to explain some of the ideas to my teammates without drawing them out on paper. However, it was an excellent opportunity for me to practice my skills in formulating and describing ideas.

Some Takeaways from this project:

  • Always keep an eye on the privacy concern of the users when dealing with personal information. Users should feel secured when entering their information into your design.
  • Always consider who are the users of your product. For example, When most of the users of your design are senior, you need to pay more attention to the accessibility and learnability of your design.
